Create a high-fidelity clickable SaaS-style web app prototype for “Christians in Politics” / “CiP”, a professional members portal for Christians in Australia who want to become more involved in politics, political parties, public service, advocacy, campaigning and civic leadership.

The product should feel modern, trustworthy, calm, professional and non-partisan. It should look like a polished SaaS membership platform, not a social media site. Use branding guidance from christiansinpolitics.com: match the existing CiP logo, colour palette, typography and tone where visible. If exact styles are unavailable, use a restrained palette with deep navy, warm white, soft gold accents and muted blue/grey UI surfaces. The design should communicate “Christian first, politics second”, “kingdom before tribe”, trust, wisdom, service, civic responsibility and theological seriousness.

Important product principle:
Members must be isolated from each other at launch. Members cannot browse, search, message, follow or publicly interact with other members. There should be no member directory, no public profiles, no comments, no likes and no open discussion forum. Communication should be between the member and CiP admins only.

Primary audience:
- Christians in Australia interested in joining a political party
- Christians already involved in politics who want support, fellowship or resources
- Christians unsure where to start politically
- Christians who want to help others get involved
- CiP administrators and coordinators

Core user journey:
1. Public landing / sign-up
2. Account creation
3. Mandatory Nicene Creed affirmation
4. If the user does not affirm the Nicene Creed, they cannot continue
5. Member dashboard
6. Complete profile
7. Optional political party guidance workflow
8. Request support from CiP
9. Register for CiP events
10. Browse resources
11. View CiP announcements and opportunities
12. Donate via external URL button

8. Optional party guidance workflow

Create a voluntary workflow for members who have not chosen a political party.

This should be clearly optional and not part of mandatory onboarding.

Entry point:
Dashboard card:
“Not sure which party to join?”
Subtext:
“Answer a short set of questions to reflect on where your views may align. This is not voting advice and CiP does not endorse any party.”

CTA:
“Start party guidance”

Workflow style:
Inspired by issue-alignment tools such as Vote Compass, but branded as a CiP reflection tool.

Screens:
1. Intro screen
2. Issue questions
3. Importance weighting
4. Personal vocation questions
5. Results summary
6. Request support

Intro screen copy:
“This tool is designed to help you reflect. It does not tell you how to vote, does not tell you which party to join, and does not represent a CiP endorsement. Your results are private unless you choose to share them with CiP when requesting support.”

Question categories:
- Economic policy
- Social policy
- Religious freedom
- Family and community
- Cost of living
- Education
- Health
- Environment and stewardship
- Immigration and multicultural Australia
- National security and defence
- Indigenous affairs
- Housing
- Public service and integrity
- Local community priorities

Question format:
Use Likert-scale cards:
- Strongly disagree
- Disagree
- Neutral / unsure
- Agree
- Strongly agree

Add optional importance weighting:
- Not important
- Somewhat important
- Very important
- Essential

Results screen:
Show a neutral visualisation:
- “Your issue alignment”
- “Areas where you appear more economically left / right”
- “Areas where you appear more socially progressive / conservative”
- “Parties you may want to research further”

Parties to include:
- Liberal Party
- Australian Labor Party
- National Party
- Australian Greens
- One Nation
- Other minor parties
- Independents

Important result disclaimer:
“This is a reflection tool, not voting advice. CiP does not endorse any party, candidate or policy. We encourage members to pray, seek wise counsel, research party platforms, and consider where they can serve faithfully.”

CTA buttons:
- “Save privately”
- “Request CiP support”
- “Explore political pathways”
- “Retake questions”

Privacy controls:
- Default result visibility: private to the member only
- User can optionally attach results to a support request
- User can delete results
